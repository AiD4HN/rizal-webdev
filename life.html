The Life of Jose Rizal

Explore the remarkable story of the Filipino national hero who changed the course of history

Life Timeline

1861

Birth in Calamba

Born José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, to Francisco Engracio Rizal Mercado y Alejandro and Teodora Morales Alonso Realonda y Quintos.

1872

Early Education

After receiving education from his mother and private tutors, Rizal was sent to study at the Ateneo Municipal de Manila where he excelled in his studies and various extracurricular activities.

1882

Journey to Spain

Departed for Spain to continue his medical studies at the Universidad Central de Madrid, where he also pursued courses in philosophy and literature.

1887

Noli Me Tángere

Published his first novel "Noli Me Tángere" (Touch Me Not) in Berlin, exposing the corruption and abuse of the Spanish colonial authorities and clergy in the Philippines.

1891

El Filibusterismo

Published his second novel "El Filibusterismo" (The Subversive) in Ghent, Belgium, which was darker than the first and called for more radical social reforms.

1892

La Liga Filipina

Returned to the Philippines and founded La Liga Filipina, a civic organization that advocated for reforms through peaceful means. Days after its establishment, Rizal was arrested and exiled to Dapitan.

1896

Execution at Bagumbayan

On December 30, 1896, Rizal was executed by firing squad at Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park) after being convicted of rebellion, sedition, and conspiracy by the Spanish colonial authorities.

"I die without seeing the dawn brighten over my native land. You who have it to see, welcome it and forget not those who have fallen during the night!" — Jose Rizal, Mi Último Adiós (My Last Farewell)

Remarkable Achievements

Literary Works

Authored two influential novels, poetry, essays, and scientific papers that exposed colonial injustices and inspired a national identity.

Polyglot

Mastered over 22 languages, including Spanish, French, Latin, Greek, German, Portuguese, Italian, English, Dutch, and Japanese.

Medicine

Studied medicine in Madrid and specialized in ophthalmology in Paris and Heidelberg. He performed the first successful cataract surgery in the Philippines.

Renaissance Man

Excelled in various fields including sculpture, painting, drawing, farming, martial arts, and physical sciences.

Personal Life

Relationships

Throughout his life, Rizal had several romantic relationships that influenced his worldview and writings. His most famous relationship was with Josephine Bracken, an Irish woman whom he met during his exile in Dapitan. Though they were unable to marry due to his refusal to return to Catholicism, they lived as husband and wife.

Family

Born to a prosperous family, Rizal was the seventh of eleven children. His parents instilled in him a deep love for education and an appreciation for Filipino culture, despite the predominant Spanish influence. His family later suffered persecution due to his activism.

Hobbies and Interests

Beyond his intellectual pursuits, Rizal enjoyed fencing, target shooting, and physical exercise. He was also known for his talent in creating sculptures and sketches. During his exile in Dapitan, he engaged in agricultural projects, established a school, and practiced medicine.
