The Life and Legacy of Jose Rizal

Jose Rizal was executed on December 30, 1896, an event that became a turning point in the Philippine revolution. In 1901, the Taft Commission officially declared him a National Hero. His death is commemorated every year on December 30.

Jose Rizal’s execution on December 30, 1896 marked a turning point in Philippine history. His death sparked a full-scale revolution that eventually led to the country’s independence in 1898. In 1901, the American-led Taft Commission officially declared Rizal a National Hero. To honor his legacy, monuments were built in many towns, and December 30 was made a national holiday. In various provinces, groups of professionals formed the Caballeros de Rizal—now known as the Knights of Rizal—to preserve and promote his ideals.
