Key Destinations

Spain (1882-1885)

First Visit: May 3, 1882

Rizal’s secret departure to Spain marked the beginning of his mission to study and observe European life, culture, laws, and government. Traveling under the name Jose Mercado, he left the Philippines on May 3, 1882, aboard the steamer Salvadora, determined to gain knowledge that would help in the struggle for Filipino freedom from Spanish rule.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Completed his medical education
  • Became active in the Propaganda Movement
  • Improved his skills in painting, sculpture, and fencing
  • Delivered speeches about the situation in the Philippines

Paris, France (1885–1886)

First Visit: Late 1885

After finishing his studies in Spain, Rizal went to Paris to further his medical specialization in ophthalmology, aiming to cure his mother’s eye condition. His time in Paris was both professionally productive and socially enriching.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Advanced his studies in ophthalmology
  • Attended scientific lectures at the University of Paris
  • Began working on his first novel "Noli Me Tángere"
  • Met and collaborated with European scientists

Germany (1886-1887)

First Visit: February 1, 1886

Rizal's time in Germany was highly productive. He continued his medical studies under prominent doctors, learned the German language, and completed his first novel. He was also influenced by German thinking and efficiency during his stay.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Published "Noli Me Tángere" in Berlin
  • Studied ophthalmology under Dr. Otto Becker
  • Became a member of the Ethnological and Anthropological Society
  • Learned the German language in just six months

England (1888)

Visit: May 1888

In London, Rizal spent his time researching at the British Museum, where he annotated Antonio de Morga's historical work "Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas." This research was crucial for his goal of highlighting the rich pre-colonial Filipino history.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Annotated Morga's "Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas"
  • Met Dr. Reinhold Rost, a prominent Orientalist
  • Improved his English language skills
  • Researched Filipino pre-colonial history

Belgium (1890)

Visit: January 28, 1890

Brussels served as Rizal's home while he worked on his second novel, "El Filibusterismo." He chose Belgium for its lower cost of living while still providing the European intellectual environment that nurtured his writing.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Wrote most of "El Filibusterismo"
  • Published articles in La Solidaridad
  • Continued his involvement with the Propaganda Movement
  • Maintained correspondence with fellow reformists

Hong Kong (1891-1892)

First Visit: November 20, 1891

After his European sojourn, Rizal settled in Hong Kong where he established a medical practice and planned his return to the Philippines. He reunited with his family who had fled persecution in their homeland.

Key Accomplishments:

  • Established a successful medical practice
  • Published "El Filibusterismo"
  • Planned the establishment of a Filipino colony in Borneo
  • Prepared for his return to the Philippines

Impact of Rizal's Travels

Rizal's extensive travels across Europe and Asia profoundly shaped his worldview and provided him with a global perspective that was rare for Filipinos of his time. His exposure to liberal ideas, scientific advancements, and various cultures directly influenced his writings and reform advocacy.

Through his journeys, Rizal was able to:

  • Gain first-hand experience of democratic societies and compare them with colonial Philippines
  • Connect with fellow reformists and intellectuals who supported Philippine reform
  • Access libraries and museums that provided him with knowledge unavailable in his homeland
  • Develop advanced skills in medicine, languages, arts, and sciences
  • Document Filipino culture and history from a perspective outside Spanish colonial influence

These experiences armed Rizal with the knowledge and perspective needed to articulate the aspirations of his countrymen and challenge the Spanish colonial narrative. His travels were not merely for personal enrichment but became a crucial element in his mission to seek justice and recognition for the Filipino people.

"Like others, I also desire the happiness of my country... I would wish that Filipinos were more critical, more steadfast, more thoughtful, more industrious, and more independent." — Jose Rizal, from a letter written abroad
